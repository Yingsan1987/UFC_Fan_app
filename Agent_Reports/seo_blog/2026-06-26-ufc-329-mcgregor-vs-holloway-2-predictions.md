# UFC 329 Predictions: McGregor vs. Holloway 2 Headlines a Card Built on History

Thirteen years after they first shared the Octagon, Conor McGregor and Max Holloway run it back. **UFC 329: McGregor vs. Holloway 2** lands on **July 11, 2026** at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas, and it might be the most-watched fight of the year. It's McGregor's first walk to the cage since he broke his leg against Dustin Poirier at UFC 264 in July 2021, and he's stepping in against a version of Holloway who is barely recognizable from the 21-year-old he beat in 2013.

Below are our UFC 329 predictions for the main event and the rest of the headline matchups, plus the storyline and odds you need before you lock in your own picks.

## UFC 329 main event: Conor McGregor vs. Max Holloway 2

### What happened the first time

The original meeting took place on August 17, 2013, at UFC Fight Night 26 in Boston. McGregor won a unanimous decision, outlanding Holloway 71 to 32 in total strikes and completing four of five takedown attempts. The wild part: McGregor tore his ACL midway through the fight and still controlled the back half on the mat to seal it. He left Boston 2-0 in the UFC and on his way to superstardom. Holloway left with the only blemish that mattered — and then spent the next decade proving it was an aberration.

### Why the rematch is so different

Holloway is no longer a prospect. After that loss he reeled off one of the most impressive featherweight runs in UFC history, captured the title, and built a reputation as one of the highest-volume, most durable strikers the sport has produced. This is a fundamentally tougher, smarter, more battle-tested opponent than the one McGregor faced in 2013 — and the fight is at **welterweight**, not featherweight.

McGregor, meanwhile, brings the most dangerous left hand in UFC history and genuine one-punch fight-ending power. But he's coming off a multi-year layoff, and layoffs plus ring rust plus a high-output opponent is a difficult combination at 170 pounds.

### The odds tell a story

For the first time in a long time, **McGregor is the underdog — reportedly the longest underdog stretch of his entire UFC career**. Lines have moved as the fight approaches and they vary meaningfully by sportsbook, but the market consistently makes Holloway the favorite. Reported numbers have ranged from Holloway in the -240 area with McGregor near +180, out to wider splits with Holloway in the -400 to -550 range and McGregor +290 to +350. Always shop your book, because the gap here is unusually wide.

### Our prediction

This is a classic power-versus-volume puzzle. If McGregor lands his left hand clean in the first two rounds, the fight is over — that's the whole reason the underdog price is as short as it is. But the longer it goes, the more Holloway's cardio, output, and chin take over. Holloway has the higher floor and the more reliable path to victory, while McGregor has the more explosive ceiling.

**Prediction:** Lean Holloway by late stoppage or decision, with a real live-dog case for an early McGregor knockout. This is exactly the kind of fight where the smart move is to make your own read and back it.

## UFC 329 co-main: Benoît Saint Denis vs. Paddy Pimblett

A genuine lightweight banger. Paddy Pimblett carries the bigger profile and a slick grappling-and-pressure game, while Benoît Saint Denis is a relentless, finish-hunting forward fighter who never stops coming. This one tends to end on the mat. **Prediction:** Whoever imposes the grappling first wins — slight lean to the fighter who can stay composed in the scramble. Expect fireworks and a strong chance of a finish.

## Cory Sandhagen vs. Mario Bautista (bantamweight)

A high-level technical bout near the top of a stacked 135-pound division. Cory Sandhagen's length, footwork, and creativity have troubled almost everyone he's faced, but Mario Bautista has quietly built a long winning streak with elite cardio and grappling. **Prediction:** Sandhagen's experience in five-round, top-contender fights gives him the edge on the feet, but don't be shocked if Bautista drags it into deep waters.

## Brandon Royval vs. Lone'er Kavanagh (flyweight)

Brandon Royval is one of the most exciting fighters in any division — high pace, scrambly, always live. Lone'er Kavanagh is a sharp, rising flyweight talent looking to announce himself against a top name. **Prediction:** Royval's championship-level experience should carry him, but this is a real test-the-prospect spot.

## Gable Steveson vs. Elisha Ellison (heavyweight)

Olympic gold-medal wrestler Gable Steveson continues his MMA development against Elisha Ellison. The athletic upside is enormous; the MMA mileage is still thin. **Prediction:** Expect Steveson to use elite wrestling to control the fight, with the only question being how polished his striking and submission defense look this time out.

## How to read UFC 329 like a sharp

A card like this rewards process over hype. A few quick reminders before you make your picks:

- **Style beats name recognition.** The biggest star isn't always the best bet — McGregor being an underdog in his own main event is proof.
- **Respect the layoff.** Long absences hurt timing and cardio more than fans expect, especially against high-volume opponents.
- **Shop the odds.** When lines swing this much between books, the number you take matters as much as the side.
- **Live dogs are where value lives.** One-punch power on a short underdog price is the definition of a live underdog.
