# UFC Baku Predictions: Fiziev vs. Torres and Full Card Picks

The UFC heads back to Azerbaijan this weekend, and the lightweight division gets a genuine crossroads main event. **UFC Fight Night: Fiziev vs. Torres** (also billed as UFC Fight Night 280) goes down Saturday, June 27, 2026, at the National Gymnastics Arena in Baku — and the headliner pits a hometown striker against one of the most explosive finishers in the 155-pound class.

Below are our UFC Baku predictions for the main event and the rest of the main card, plus the odds, the storylines that matter, and where each fight could swing. ## UFC Baku: How to watch and what's at stake

This is the UFC's second trip to Baku, and the promotion has signed a deal to run a Fight Night in the city every year through 2028 — so the local crowd has plenty of reason to show up loud. Prelims are scheduled to begin at noon ET with the main card following at 3 p.m. ET, streaming on Paramount+.

The hook in the main event is simple: **Rafael Fiziev is fighting at home.** Born in Baku, the Kazakhstan-based striker gets a rare home-soil headliner, and he badly needs a win. Across from him stands **Manuel "El Loco" Torres**, a Mexican knockout artist riding genuine momentum up the lightweight ladder. Both men are ranked contenders in a division that is wide open behind the champion, and a finish here puts the winner back in the conversation near the top 10.

## Fiziev vs. Torres prediction: the main event

Let's start with the records and recent form. **Rafael Fiziev (13-5)** is one of the most technically polished strikers in the division — a Muay Thai specialist with brutal leg kicks, sharp counters, and the kind of distance management that has troubled almost everyone he's faced. The problem is his recent results: he sits at 1-2 over his last three, most recently dropping a TKO loss to Mauricio Ruffy on January 31, 2026. Knee injuries and tough matchups have stalled what once looked like a title run.

**Manuel Torres (17-3)** is the opposite kind of story — a fighter trending up. He's gone 2-1 in his last three, headlined by a December 6, 2025 win over the durable Grant Dawson. Torres earns his nickname honestly: he throws heavy leather, hunts the finish, and has rarely seen his fights reach the scorecards. Almost all of his wins have come by stoppage, which tells you exactly how he wants this to go.

That contrast is the whole fight. Fiziev is the cleaner, more disciplined technician who wants to control range, chop the lead leg, and pile up rounds. Torres wants chaos — short exchanges, heavy hands, and a moment where Fiziev's chin gets tested. Notably, the betting line reflects how close this is: oddsmakers have opened Fiziev as only a slight favorite (around **-115** at DraftKings), essentially a coin flip.

**Our pick:** Fiziev by decision — but cautiously. The home crowd, the five-round format, and his superior volume and footwork should let him bank rounds if he stays disciplined and avoids the wild firefight Torres craves. The live underdog case is real, though: if Torres lands clean early, Fiziev's recent durability questions make a Torres knockout very much in play. This is a fight where a confident pick can pay off big, so weigh the risk before you commit.

## UFC Baku main card predictions

### Shara Magomedov vs. Michel Pereira (Middleweight)

The co-main is a showcase of two of the most watchable strikers in the sport. **Shara "Bullet" Magomedov** brings an unorthodox, point-fighting karate style and crowd-pleasing spinning attacks, while **Michel Pereira** is one of the most creative — and unpredictable — athletes on the roster. Expect highlight-reel attempts from both. Pereira's grappling and cardio edge could be the difference if he chooses to wrestle, but in a pure striking match this is must-watch. **Lean: Pereira**, with a real chance Shara steals it on flash and accuracy.

### Nazim Sadykhov vs. Matheus Camilo (Lightweight)

A regional crowd-pleaser. **Nazim Sadykhov** is an aggressive, finish-minded lightweight who will be fighting close to a friendly crowd, while **Matheus Camilo** brings the pressure and submission threat common to the Brazilian contingent. **Lean: Sadykhov** in a competitive scrap.

### Asu Almabayev vs. Charles Johnson (Flyweight)

A strong test of grappling versus movement. **Asu Almabayev** is a relentless, high-pace wrestler who smothers opponents and racks up control time. **Charles Johnson** is a rangy, technical striker who needs to keep this standing. If Almabayev gets his takedowns, he likely grinds out the decision. **Lean: Almabayev.**

### Brunno Ferreira vs. Ikram Aliskerov (Middleweight)

Two heavy hitters who don't tend to leave it to the judges. **Ikram Aliskerov** combines power with strong Dagestani grappling and is looking to bounce back among the middleweight contenders, while **Brunno "Hulk" Ferreira** carries genuine one-punch knockout power. This one likely doesn't last long. **Lean: Aliskerov**, but caution — Ferreira can end any fight in a single exchange.

### Abusupiyan Magomedov vs. Michał Oleksiejczuk

Originally floated as a possible headliner before the Fiziev–Torres booking, this matchup of physical, aggressive finishers landed on the main card and should deliver fireworks. Both men fight at a high pace and look to hurt opponents early — a strong candidate for a Fight of the Night bonus.

## How to use these UFC Baku predictions

A few things worth remembering before you build out a full card prediction. First, lightweight is volatile — Torres's finishing rate means the main event can flip in a single round, so don't over-anchor on the favorite. Second, several of these fights feature heavy hitters (Ferreira, Aliskerov, Oleksiejczuk) where "method of victory" picks can be far more rewarding than just picking a winner. And third, the home-crowd factor in Baku is real for fighters like Fiziev and Sadykhov — energy matters in close rounds.

Odds and matchups can shift right up until fight night with weigh-ins and any late changes, so check the latest card before locking anything in.
