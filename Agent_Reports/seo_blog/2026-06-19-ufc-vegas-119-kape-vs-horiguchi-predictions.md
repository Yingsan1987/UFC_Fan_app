# UFC Vegas 119 Predictions: Kape vs. Horiguchi and Full Card Picks

The flyweight division gets a defining night on Saturday, June 20, when Manel Kape and Kyoji Horiguchi headline UFC Fight Night 279 (UFC Vegas 119) from the Meta APEX in Las Vegas. It is a fight nine years in the making, a likely title eliminator, and one of the most intriguing 125-pound matchups the UFC has put together this year. ## The stakes: a flyweight title eliminator in all but name

UFC flyweight is in a fascinating spot. Joshua Van climbed to the top of the division when he dethroned Alexandre Pantoja at UFC 323 on December 6, 2025 — a strange ending, with Pantoja suffering an injury inside the opening 30 seconds. Pantoja is widely expected to get an immediate rematch when he is healthy, which means the rest of the division is jostling for whatever title shot opens up next.

That is exactly why Kape vs. Horiguchi matters. Both men sit inside the top five, and the winner walks away with the strongest claim to the next available crack at gold. Kape himself has framed it that way: by reports, he could have waited for a shot but chose Horiguchi to make a statement and "scare the division." Whoever wins on Saturday positions themselves at the front of a crowded contender line that also includes names like Tatsuro Taira, Asu Almabayev, and the Brandons — Moreno and Royval.

## The history: a rematch nine years later

This is not the first meeting between these two. Back in 2017, Kape and Horiguchi crossed paths in the semifinals of the RIZIN Fighting World Grand Prix in Saitama, Japan. Horiguchi got the better of it that night, submitting Kape with an arm-triangle choke in the third round.

Both fighters are nearly unrecognizable from those versions of themselves. Kape has rebuilt into one of the most explosive strikers in the division, while Horiguchi remains one of the most accomplished 135- and 125-pound fighters of his generation, with a résumé that spans the UFC, RIZIN, and Bellator. Nine years of evolution makes the 2017 result close to irrelevant — but the personal edge is real, and Horiguchi has openly vowed to beat Kape again to earn his shot.

## Kape vs. Horiguchi: the tale of the tape

Manel Kape enters as the higher-ranked man at flyweight and the betting favorite, with most books listing him in the range of -162 to -170 at time of writing. Horiguchi sits as the underdog around +132. Those numbers reflect a close fight that oddsmakers narrowly lean toward Kape — not a runaway.

**Manel Kape's path to victory** runs through his striking. He is a violent, twitchy counter-puncher with genuine knockout power for the weight class and some of the fastest hands in the division. If he can keep the fight standing and at range, his speed and finishing instinct give him the clearest route to a highlight-reel ending.

**Kyoji Horiguchi's path to victory** is built on pace, footwork, and championship-level experience. Horiguchi is famously well-rounded, blending crisp boxing with a strong wrestling and submission base — the same toolkit that submitted Kape the first time. If he can force exchanges, mix in level changes, and drag Kape into deep water, his cardio and craft become major factors in the championship rounds.

### Our main event prediction

This one is close, and the odds reflect it. Kape's power is the great equalizer — one clean shot can end any flyweight fight — and his improved defensive discipline makes him harder to time than he was earlier in his UFC run. But Horiguchi's volume, sequencing, and tactical IQ are problems that explosive strikers have struggled to solve. Expect a tense, momentum-swinging fight. Our lean is **Kape by decision or late stoppage**, but a Horiguchi grind to a clear-cut decision would surprise no one. This is a genuine coin-flip headliner — the kind that rewards fans who actually study the matchup before picking.

## UFC Vegas 119 full card predictions

The rest of the card is stacked with action fights and a few lopsided lines worth noting. Predictions below are based on current matchups and reported odds.

### Co-main event: Navajo Stirling vs. Ion Cutelaba (Light Heavyweight)

Rising prospect Navajo Stirling is a heavy favorite (around -340) against the always-dangerous veteran Ion Cutelaba (+248). Cutelaba's chaotic, all-out early pressure is a real threat in round one, but Stirling's size, length, and composure should carry him if he weathers the storm. **Prediction: Stirling, but don't sleep on Cutelaba landing early.**

### Christian Rodriguez vs. Hyder Amil (Featherweight)

Christian Rodriguez (-184) is the more proven, well-rounded fighter against the unbeaten-minded striker Hyder Amil (+143). Rodriguez's wrestling and ability to control the pace make him the pick. **Prediction: Rodriguez by decision.**

### Vinicius Oliveira vs. Andre Fili (Featherweight)

The dynamic Vinicius Oliveira (around -319) is favored over crafty veteran Andre Fili (+234). Oliveira's creativity and finishing ability should shine, though Fili's experience always makes him live. **Prediction: Oliveira.**

### Melsik Baghdasaryan vs. Murtazali Magomedov (Featherweight)

A striker-versus-grappler clash with Magomedov favored. **Prediction: Magomedov if he gets it to the mat; Baghdasaryan if it stays standing.**

### Allan Nascimento vs. Mitch Raposo (Flyweight)

A competitive 125-pound scrap, with Nascimento the slight favorite on the grappling side. **Prediction: Nascimento by close decision.**

These are reads, not certainties — that is the fun of fight night. The fans who consistently call the upsets are usually the ones who do their homework on the matchups, not just the names.
